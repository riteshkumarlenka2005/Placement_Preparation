## 1. 🛑 The "Indianisms" to Avoid (Common Mistakes)

"Indianisms" are literal translations from our native languages to English that sound incorrect to professional recruiters. 

| ❌ What we usually say (Incorrect) | ✅ What you SHOULD say (Professional) |
|------------------------------------|---------------------------------------|
| "I am having 2 years of experience." | "I **have** 2 years of experience." |
| "I did a mistake." | "I **made** a mistake." |
| "Please revert back to my email." | "Please **revert** to my email." *(Revert already means to reply back)* |
| "Let us discuss about the project." | "Let us **discuss** the project." *(Discuss means to talk about)* |
| "I passed out of college in 2026." | "I **graduated** from college in 2026." *(Passed out means to faint/lose consciousness!)* |
| "Myself Ritesh." | "I am Ritesh." or "My name is Ritesh." |
| "Do one thing..." | "Could you please..." or "Here is what we can do..." |

## 2. ⏳ Tenses (How to Use Them in Interviews)

You don't need a PhD in grammar. You only need to master these 3 tenses for interviews:

### A. Simple Past (For Projects & Experience)
Use this when talking about things you have **already finished**.
* **Formula:** Subject + V2 (Past tense verb)
* **Interview Use:** "Tell me about your project."
* **Example:** "I **developed** a web application using React. I **optimized** the database, which **reduced** loading time by 20%."
* ❌ *Mistake:* Don't say "I have developed" for a project completed 2 years ago. Just say "I developed".

### B. Simple Present (For Skills & Habits)
Use this for universal truths, daily habits, and your current skills.
* **Formula:** Subject + V1 (Base verb)
* **Interview Use:** "What are your skills?"
* **Example:** "I **code** in Java. I **understand** data structures very well."
* ❌ *Mistake:* Don't say "I am coding in Java" unless you are literally typing code right at that exact second.

### C. Present Perfect (For Achievements with Current Value)
Use this for things you did in the past, but the result is important *right now*.
* **Formula:** Subject + has/have + V3
* **Interview Use:** "Why should we hire you?"
* **Example:** "I **have solved** over 200 questions on LeetCode." (Shows you are currently prepared).

## 3. 🤝 Subject-Verb Agreement (The Golden Rule)

This is the #1 reason candidates fail written English/Verbal Aptitude tests.

**The Golden Rule:** 
- Singular Subject = Singular Verb (usually ends in 's')
- Plural Subject = Plural Verb (does not end in 's')

**Examples:**
* ✅ "The **company offers** great benefits." (Company is singular, offers gets an 's')
* ✅ "The **companies offer** great benefits." (Companies is plural, offer has no 's')
* ✅ "Ritesh **writes** code." (Singular)
* ✅ "The developers **write** code." (Plural)

**Tricky Cases (Always tested in aptitude exams):**
1. **Everyone, Everyone, Nobody, Someone** are ALWAYS singular.
   * ❌ "Everyone *are* happy."
   * ✅ "Everyone **is** happy."
2. **Either/Or, Neither/Nor** - The verb matches the noun *closest* to it.
   * ✅ "Neither the manager nor the **employees are** here."
   * ✅ "Neither the employees nor the **manager is** here."

## 4. 🚀 Upgrade Your Vocabulary (Basic ➡️ Advanced)

Swap out simple words for these power words in your resume and interviews:

| Basic Word | Power Word (Use this!) | Example in an Interview |
|------------|------------------------|-------------------------|
| Did / Made | **Executed / Developed** | "I **developed** a new algorithm." |
| Good | **Proficient / Strong** | "I am **proficient** in Python." |
| Hard | **Challenging** | "It was a **challenging** bug to fix." |
| Used | **Leveraged / Utilized** | "I **leveraged** Git for version control." |
| Fixed | **Resolved** | "I **resolved** the server downtime issue." |
| Teamwork | **Collaboration** | "I believe in strong cross-functional **collaboration**." |

## 5. 📧 Email Etiquette (Bonus)

When you email HR or your manager, keep it crisp.

**Good Subject Lines:**
* `Application for Software Engineer Role - Ritesh Kumar Lenka`
* `Query Regarding Offer Letter - Ritesh Kumar Lenka`

**Good Openings:**
* "Dear Hiring Manager,"
* "Hi [First Name]," (If the company culture is casual)

**Good Closings:**
* "Best regards,"
* "Sincerely,"
* "Looking forward to hearing from you,"
